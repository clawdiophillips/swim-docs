# Markers

Markers are time-stamped pins you place on a video frame to capture a specific moment in a race or training swim. SwimAI uses markers to calculate splits, measure underwater distance, and identify technical events.

---

## Marker Types

SwimAI has seven built-in marker types, each with a distinct colour and icon:

| Marker | Colour | Keyboard shortcut | When to use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Start** | Blue | `S` | The instant the swimmer's feet leave the block (or the push-off for backstroke) |
| **Turn** | Orange | `T` | The instant the swimmer's hand (or feet) touches the wall at a turn |
| **Breakout** | Green | `B` | The first stroke cycle after a turn or start, when the head breaks the surface |
| **Finish** | Blue | `F` | The instant the swimmer's hand touches the finish wall |
| **15m / Underwater** | Purple | `U` | The 15m mark — used to measure underwater distance off start or turns |
| **Reaction** | Red | `R` | Reaction time events (starter's signal to start of movement) |
| **Custom** | Grey | `M` | Any other event you want to annotate (e.g. stroke error, equipment failure) |

For a deeper reference, see [Marker Types →](/docs/reference/marker-types)

## Adding a Marker

1. Open a race in the annotate screen
2. Pause the video on the exact frame you want to mark
3. Press the keyboard shortcut (e.g. `T` for a turn), or click **+ Add** in the marker panel and choose the type

The marker is placed at the current playhead position and immediately appears in the panel and on the timeline.

<Callout type="tip">
  Use the **← →** arrow keys to step one frame at a time before placing a marker. At 60fps each frame is ~16ms, so frame accuracy matters for competitive splits.
</Callout>

---

## Editing a Marker

### Move a marker to a different frame

1. In the marker panel, click the marker you want to move
2. Scrub the video to the correct frame
3. Click **Update Time** (the clock icon next to the marker) or press `Enter`

Alternatively, drag the pin on the timeline left or right.

### Rename a marker

Double-click the marker's name in the panel to make it editable. Type the new name and press `Enter`.

This is useful for multi-athlete videos — for example, "Turn 1 – Athlete A" and "Turn 1 – Athlete B".

---

## Deleting a Marker

- Hover over the marker row in the panel and click the **trash icon**, or
- Click the marker to select it and press the **Delete** key

<Callout type="warning">
  Deleting a marker cannot be undone. If you delete a Turn marker, the splits that depend on it will recalculate automatically.
</Callout>

---

## Ordering and Grouping

Markers are always displayed in **chronological order** — sorted by timestamp, not by the order you created them.

If you have multiple swimmers in one video, use Custom markers with descriptive names to distinguish events. For a cleaner workflow with multi-swimmer footage, consider the [Comparison](/docs/features/comparison) feature instead.

---

## Marker Notes

You can attach a text note to any marker:

1. Click the marker in the panel to expand it
2. Click the **note** field (pencil icon)
3. Type your observation — e.g. "Late touch, slightly crooked"
4. Press `Enter` to save

Notes appear in the exported CSV and in the shareable annotation link.
